What Snapchat Actually Collects About You in 2026

The privacy policy is 8,000 words. These are the bullets that affect your daily life.

PS
Priya Sharma Privacy editor
Jun 16, 2026 10 min read

Snapchat's business model is free entertainment funded by ads. That sentence tells you most of what you need to know about what data they want — the details are about which sensors and signals feed the ad engine.

Data categories worth caring about

Account basics: phone number, email, birthday, display name. Required to function.

Content metadata: who you message, how often you open the app, which lenses you try. Powers engagement ranking and ad frequency.

Location: optional but valuable. Snap Map with Ghost Mode off reveals precise coordinates to friends — and generates place-based ad segments.

Device signals: model, OS version, ad identifier, IP address. Standard across mobile apps.

Inferred interests: built from lenses used, content watched, websites you visit off-platform if tracking is enabled elsewhere.

Stories disappear from the UI in 24 hours. Snap's servers may retain copies longer for legal, safety, and analytics reasons — read their retention schedule if that gap matters to you.

How long data sticks around

Ephemeral messaging is Snapchat's brand promise, but "ephemeral" applies to the consumer experience more than the backend. Account logs, purchase records, and abuse reports stick around on different timelines. You can request your data export in Settings to see the raw dump.

Reducing your footprint — realistically

  • Enable Ghost Mode by default
  • Turn off Activity-Based Ads in Ad Preferences
  • Revoke unnecessary iOS/Android permissions (contacts, precise location)
  • Use Login Verification
  • Delete old Memories you no longer need
  • Audit third-party apps connected via Snapchat Login

Third-party download tools

When you use external sites, you add another party to the chain. Choose tools with clear privacy policies and minimal data collection. SnapSaver does not require accounts and does not store download history — but your browser and network still see the request, as with any site.
