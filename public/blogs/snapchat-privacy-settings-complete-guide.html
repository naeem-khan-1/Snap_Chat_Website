Snapchat Privacy Settings: The Complete Guide for 2026

Most people set up Snapchat once and never look back. These are the settings worth revisiting — especially if strangers can find you.

PS
Priya Sharma Privacy editor
Jun 18, 2026 10 min read

Snapchat buries its privacy controls three menus deep. I spent an afternoon clicking through every toggle on a test account so you do not have to — and a few of them genuinely surprised me.

The app defaults lean toward openness: Quick Add suggests you to strangers, Snap Map can broadcast your location, and story visibility often starts wider than people expect. None of that is hidden, but it is easy to miss when you are racing through onboarding.

Start here: who can contact you

Settings → Contact Me controls whether anyone, friends only, or nobody can send you snaps and chats. If you get random messages, set this to Friends Only. It is the single highest-impact change for reducing harassment.

Story visibility

Settings → View My Story offers Everyone, Friends Only, or a custom list. Public profiles are a different beast — they are meant to be discoverable — but personal accounts should almost always use Friends Only unless you are actively trying to grow an audience.

Privacy settings stop strangers from seeing your story in the app. They do not stop a friend from screenshotting. Share accordingly.

Location and Snap Map

Ghost Mode is your friend. When enabled, your live location disappears from the map entirely. If you want select people to see you, turn Ghost Mode off but trim the list to actual close friends — not your entire contact book.

The settings people forget

  • Quick Add — limits whether you appear in "people you may know" suggestions
  • See Me in Quick Add — flip off if you want a lower profile
  • Ad Preferences — under Additional Services; reduces activity-based targeting
  • Login Verification — two-factor by SMS or authenticator app
  • Memories passcode — locks saved snaps behind a PIN

Public profile? Read this twice

Once you create a public profile, your stories and spotlights are built for discovery — including through web tools that read public data. If that makes you uncomfortable, skip the public profile entirely or post only content you are fine with the internet seeing.

Quick audit: Open Settings right now, search "privacy," and spend five minutes on Contact Me, View My Story, and Ghost Mode. That covers 80% of problems I see in reader emails.
